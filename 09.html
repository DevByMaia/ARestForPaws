The Pythons’ Guide to Living Their Best Lives

March 16, 2024

Welcome to the world of ball pythons—where the temperature is just right, the humidity is on point, and your reptilian friend is always one shed away from looking fabulous. Caring for these cold-blooded creatures is not for everyone, but it’s a noble pursuit nonetheless.

The Perfect Habitat

When setting up the habitat, remember that multiple ball pythons in the same tank is not advisable. These snakes prefer solitude, which means sharing a home could lead to stress and the potential for unwanted diseases.

For hatchlings, a cozy 10-gallon tank will do, but don’t think bigger is better; stress levels can skyrocket in oversized homes. Upgrade to a 20-gallon when they hit juvenile stage (about 3 months old), and for the adults (at least 3 years old), aim for a spacious 30-40 gallon abode. Inside their tank, create a cozy atmosphere with a warm side (80-90°F) and a cool side (70-80°F). Nighttime should bring a chill, keeping things at a cool 70-80°F.

Decor Essentials

Now that we have the basics down, let’s talk about enrichment. Think tree branches, stones, and hidey-holes—your ball python’s version of a luxurious condo with amenities. For physical enrichment, throw in some perches and bridges. For mental stimulation, consider puzzles and mazes where they have to work for their food. Just remember, it’s not a day at the spa if you throw in strong-smelling items like perfumes; keep things natural with lemon peels and spices instead.

Heating Things Up

Now, here’s where the real magic happens: snakes are cold-blooded, meaning they rely on external sources to maintain their body temperature. A low-wattage heat lamp is a must— just make sure it’s positioned above the tank on a mesh or wire screen. And don’t forget the timer to recreate day and night cycles.

Water: The Elixir of Life

Snakes aren’t big drinkers. A few of times a week, they’ll want to hydrate, and that’s why you need to provide them with a clean water dish—clean it every other day, or you might as well be offering them a mud puddle. They love to soak, too, so ensure the dish is large enough for them to lounge in like they just returned from a spa day.

The Shedding Saga

Every 4-6 weeks, your ball python will embark on the most dramatic phase of their life: shedding. To assist them, provide lukewarm water for soaking and toss in some rubbing objects— tree branches and stones. It’s like giving them a massage during their very own beauty treatment.

Feeding Time: The Great Rodent Debate

When it comes to feeding your ball python, opt for pre-killed rodents (fresh or frozen, take your pick). It’s more humane, and it prevents live rodents from turning your snake’s mealtime into a horror film. Young ball pythons get two meals a week, while the adults can space it out to once every 1-2 weeks. And when it’s time to feed, ensure the size of the rodent is roughly the same size—or slightly larger than—the snake's midsection.

Reading Body Language

Finally, it’s crucial to understand your snake’s feelings. A content ball python flicks its tongue and slithers about with a relaxed grip—think of it as the equivalent of a satisfied sigh. On the other hand, a nervous python might skip dinner, hiss in disapproval, or rub against objects in its tank. And if you’ve got an aggressive one? Hissing, striking, and an “S” shaped posture are signs that they’re not exactly rolling out the welcome mat.

When approaching your serpent, remember the golden rule: wash your hands! Snakes have a knack for sniffing out rodent odors, and they might mistake your fingers for dinner. Approach them calmly from the side, and if needed, use a snake hook to guide them—never from above, lest you become a startled fly in a web of chaos.

Hold them with both hands—one to support their body and the other their head—watching for any signs of discomfort. Don't overstay your welcome; keep handling sessions short and sweet, especially if they’ve just eaten or are in shedding mode. Allow them to explore in your grasp—this is their kingdom, after all.

With a little diligence and a lot of love, you can create a serene sanctuary for your ball python. Just remember, they may not return the sentiment in the same way a dog would, but I assure you, nothing feels quite as rewarding as seeing your snake flick its tongue in contentment—a silent thank you for all your hard work in making their life just a little bit better.
